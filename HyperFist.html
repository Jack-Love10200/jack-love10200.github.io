HYPERFIST - Implementation Highlights

HYPERFIST is a fast-pased first person beat-em-up that I joined halfway through development to help with graphics work. It leans heavily on an over-the-top comic book/cartoon art style, bold colors, sharp silhouettes and exaggerated animations. My main contributions on this project were implementing impact frames to enhance the feel of big punches, adding support for steam features (achievements, leaderboards) as well as preparing the game for release on Steam Deck.

Outline material

All of the math needed to create a dynamic outline of any mesh.

One of the most common techniques for creating Dynamic outlines around objects is to use a duplicate of the mesh with all of it's normals facing inward and extrude everything outwards. This is what my outline material does, with the added step of flattening the mesh so that multiple outlines could be stacked and moving a few of the vertices out more than the rest to create irregular spikes.

Environment Lines & Background

The next question is what to do with the background enviornment during the impact. The references I was using washed out the background with a single strong color, making the image more striking and less visually complex. I decieded to take a similar approach, washing out the background while keeping outlines of the background elements as to not lose all positional reference, which could be disorienting in a game.

Death Spray

This effect was primarily inspired by the death effect in the game "I Am Your Beast", in which a stylies X appears over top of enemies as they are defeated, thought the primary reference materials also use a similar effect.

"I Am Your Beast" Death Effect Inspiration

Impact frame with death spray

HYPERFIST already has a set of spray paint tags strewn throughout walls of the levels. Many of them were perfect to repurpose to go over enemies faces if they were killed during an impact frame.

Existing Sprays

Two of the many spray paint tags made for HYPERFIST

During an impact, the impact frame manager checks if any of the enemies involved will die from the hit, and if so, spawns a death spray at the position of their skeleton's head socket. Billboarding (rotating to face the camera) this object and giving it a depth offset so that it renders on top of everything else puts it nicely over the enemy's head when they die.

Additional Details

The shape of the outlines is nice, but they could use more complexity. Hatching lines and halftone dots fit with HYPERFIST's comic style, and break up the silhouette enough to make it a bit more visually interesting. To implement this, I followed the math from This GDC Presentation on Signed Distance fields in HI-FI Rush and implemented it into a material function.

Paper Texture Added

Recedes the background and adds texture

At this point, the impact frames look pretty good in terms of shape, but they could be punchier in action. Inverting the colors for the first 3 frames (on ones) of the impact is a technique that added a lot more of a jolt in the references I used, and is super simple to implement as (pure white - original color)^2.

Hatching Lines/Halftone Dots Added

Helps break up detail to the silhouette

Flat colors don't scream "polish", multiplying paper textures into the outlines and background during the post process step is a simple way to make the whole effect feel more cohesive. Muliplying in different textures/ammounts also serves to seperated the character and outlines from the background more.
